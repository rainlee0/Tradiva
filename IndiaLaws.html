India

A South Asian country known for its diverse culture, ancient history, and vibrant traditions. Home to landmarks like the Taj Mahal and Jaipur’s palaces, India is also famous for its spicy cuisine, Bollywood films, and colorful festivals like Diwali and Holi.

⚖️10 Weird Laws in India:

  • •💰It’s Illegal to Carry More Than ₹10 in Nepal Without Declaration!
    you cannot carry more than ₹10 in Indian currency without declaring it to customs. This law aims to prevent illegal money transactions between the two countries.
  • •🚫No Kissing in Public
    Public displays of affection, like kissing or hugging, are considered offensive in many parts of India.
  • •🪁You Need a Special License to Fly a Kite
    Ikites are considered "aircraft," meaning you technically need a license to fly one. Though not strictly enforced, the law still exists!
  • •🏦You Can’t Take More Than ₹50,000 in Coins
    IUnder the Coinage Act (2011), carrying more than ₹50,000 in coins is illegal, as it can be considered an attempt to hoard currency.
  • •📚You Can Get Arrested for Cheating in Exams
    In some Indian states like Bihar, if you are caught cheating in an exam, you can face jail time. Authorities take academic integrity very seriously!
  • •🐄It's Illegal to Own a Cattle Without Government Permission!
    Since cows are considered sacred animals in India, you cannot legally own one without government permission in certain states
  • •💵It’s Illegal to Take Indian Currency Out of the Country!
    According to Indian customs laws, foreigners cannot take Indian Rupees (INR) out of the country. Even Indian citizens have strict limits on carrying cash abroad
  • •🚦You Can Be Fined for Not Letting a Cow Cross the Road
    In many parts of India, cows roam freely on the streets. If you honk at or disturb a cow crossing the road, you could be fined or even arrested!
  • •👑You Can’t Name Your Child “Rani” in Some Regions
    The name “Rani” (meaning “Queen”) is restricted in some regions as it was historically used only by royalty
  • •⚖️If a Man Promises Marriage and Breaks It, He Can Be Sued
    Under Indian law, if a man promises marriage and then backs out, the woman can file a case for fraud.
