Greece

Greece is a land of rich history and breathtaking landscapes, home to iconic landmarks like the Parthenon, Santorini’s white-washed houses, and the crystal-clear waters of the Aegean Sea. Known for its mythology, philosophy, and delicious Mediterranean cuisine, Greece’s culture reflects a deep connection to its ancient heritage and vibrant traditions.

🇬🇷 10 Essential Travel Tips for Greece

  • •📖 Learn Basic Greek Phrases
    While many Greeks speak English, especially in tourist areas, locals appreciate when visitors use basic Greek phrases.
  • •🍽️ Greeks Eat Late – Don’t Rush Dinner
    Dinner in Greece usually starts after 8:00 PM, and it's common for people to eat as late as 10:00 PM or later.
  • •💰 Cash is King – Many Places Don’t Accept Cards
    Many small shops, family-run restaurants, and taxis only accept cash.
  • •🚖 Taxis Don’t Always Use Meters
    Some taxi drivers may try to overcharge tourists by not using the meter.
  • •🚰 Be Careful with Tap Water on the Islands
    While tap water in Athens and major cities is safe to drink, many Greek islands rely on desalinated water, which may not taste great.
  • •⛪ Respect Religious Sites – Cover Shoulders and Knees
    When visiting Greek Orthodox churches and monasteries, both men and women should dress modestly.
  • •👥 Greeks Are Very Social – Expect Personal Questions
    Greeks love socializing and may ask personal questions like “Are you married?” or “Do you have kids?” These are meant to show interest, not invade privacy.
  • •⚠️ Be Prepared for Strikes and Protests
    Strikes, especially in public transport and ferries, happen frequently in Greece, sometimes with little notice.
  • •🚽 Don’t Flush Toilet Paper
    Greek plumbing systems in many islands and old buildings are narrow, meaning toilet paper can cause blockages.
  • •☀️ Watch Out for Heat in the Summer
    Greek summers can be extremely hot, with temperatures reaching 40°C (104°F) or higher.
