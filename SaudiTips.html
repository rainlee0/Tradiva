Saudi Arabia: Where Tradition Meets Modernity Saudi Arabia blends rich traditions with rapid modernization. It is home to landmarks like the Holy Kaaba in Mecca, Riyadh’s skyscrapers, and the vast Empty Quarter desert. Known for its Arabian hospitality and traditional dishes like Kabsa, the country’s customs reflect its deep Islamic and cultural heritage.

10 Essential Travel Tips for Saudi Arabia 🇸🇦:

  • •👗Dress Modestly, Especially in Public Places
    Saudi Arabia follows conservative dress codes, especially for women. While foreign women are not required to wear an abaya, dressing modestly is expected.
  • •📆Weekends Are Friday and Saturday
    Unlike Western countries, the Saudi weekend is on Friday and Saturday, with Friday being the Islamic holy day.
  • •🕌Respect Prayer Times
    Muslims pray five times a day, and in Saudi Arabia, many shops, restaurants, and businesses close during prayer times.
  • •🚫Public Displays of Affection Are Not Allowed
    Holding hands, hugging, and kissing in public are not culturally acceptable in Saudi Arabia.
  • •🚖Taxis and Ride-Sharing Apps Are the Best Transport
    Public transport is limited in many Saudi cities, so taxis and ride-hailing apps like Uber and Careem
  • •🍷No Alcohol Allowed
    Alcohol is completely banned in Saudi Arabia, and attempting to bring it into the country or consume it is illegal.
  • •🚻Women Should Use Designated Areas in Public Spaces
    Some restaurants, parks, and government offices have separate areas for men and families.
  • •📸Avoid Photographing People Without Permission
    Taking photos of people, especially women, and government buildings without permission is considered disrespectful and may be illegal.
  • •⚠️Avoid Public Criticism of the Government
    Speaking negatively about the Saudi government, leaders, or religion in public or online can lead to legal consequences.
  • •🌙Respect Local Customs During Ramadan
    During the holy month of Ramadan, eating, drinking, or smoking in public during daylight hours is not allowed.
