Japan

An East Asian country known for its blend of ancient traditions and cutting-edge technology. Home to landmarks like Mount Fuji and Tokyo’s Shibuya Crossing, Japan is also famous for its sushi, samurai history, cherry blossoms, and unique pop culture, including anime and manga.

⚖️10 Weird Laws in Japan:

  • •⚖️It’s Illegal to Be Overweight!
    Japan has a "Metabo Law", which requires companies to measure the waistlines of employees aged 40-74. If someone exceeds the limit (85 cm for men, 90 cm for women), they must take health counseling.
  • •💃No Dancing After Midnight!
    dancing in clubs after midnight was illegal unless the venue had a special permit
  • •🚭No Walking and Smoking!
    Smoking while walking in public places is banned in many Japanese cities.
  • •🛁You Can’t Bathe in a Hot Spring With Tattoos!
    Many onsen (hot springs) in Japan do not allow people with tattoos, as tattoos are associated with the Yakuza (Japanese mafia)
  • •🎵No Loud Car Music!
    Playing loud music in your car in residential areas is considered a public nuisance and can lead to fines.
  • •🗑️No Trash Cans in Public!
    Japan has very few trash cans in public spaces due to strict waste management rules.
  • •🗺️It’s Illegal to Make Fake Maps!
    Japan has strict mapping laws, and altering maps or creating misleading maps is illegal.
  • •🍽️No Splitting Bills in Some Restaurants!
    Some traditional Japanese restaurants do not allow customers to split the bill, expecting one person to pay for the entire meal
  • •😴Sleeping on the Job is Encouraged!
    In Japan, "Inemuri" (sleeping at work) is seen as a sign of hard work and dedication. As long as you don’t fully doze off, it’s considered acceptable!
