Saudi Arabia: Where Tradition Meets Modernity Saudi Arabia blends rich traditions with rapid modernization. It is home to landmarks like the Holy Kaaba in Mecca, Riyadh’s skyscrapers, and the vast Empty Quarter desert. Known for its Arabian hospitality and traditional dishes like Kabsa, the country’s customs reflect its deep Islamic and cultural heritage.

⚖️10 Weird Laws in Saudi Arabia:

  • •💔No Valentine’s Day Celebrations!
    For many years, Valentine’s Day was banned in Saudi Arabia, and stores were not allowed to sell red roses, gifts, or decorations.
  • •🔊No Loud Music in Public Places
    In many areas of Saudi Arabia, playing loud music in public spaces is discouraged, especially near mosques or during prayer times.
  • • 🚗🚫Women Couldn’t Drive Until 2018 (Past Law)
    Before 2018, women were not allowed to drive in Saudi Arabia, making it the only country in the world
  • •⚠️Public Criticism of the Government is Illegal
    Speaking negatively about the Saudi government, leaders, or religion in public or online can lead to serious legal consequences.
  • •🚻Gender Segregation in Some Public Places
    Traditionally, Saudi Arabia had strict gender segregation laws, especially in restaurants, schools, and workplaces
  • •👚Women Must Cover Their Shoulders in Public Places
    While women are no longer required to wear an abaya, they are still expected to dress modestly
  • •🎬No Cinemas Until 2018
    For 35 years, cinemas were banned in Saudi Arabia. In 2018, the government lifted the ban
  • •📸No Taking Photos of Government Buildings or Palaces
    Taking pictures of government buildings, military sites, or royal palaces is strictly prohibited in Saudi Arabia and can lead to fines.
  • •🌙No Eating in Public During Ramadan Before Iftar
    During the holy month of Ramadan, eating, drinking, or smoking in public before sunset (Iftar) is not allowed
  • •🐵It’s Illegal to Own a Pet Monkey Without a Permit
    Saudi Arabia has strict animal regulations, and owning wild animals like monkeys or exotic pets requires a special government permit.
