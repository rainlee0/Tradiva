South Korea

Korea: A country in East Asia known for its rich culture and history. South Korea is famous for its technology, K-pop, and cuisine, while North Korea is known for its closed political system.

Capital: Seoul   Currency: Won   Continent: Asia

Korean Food

Kimbap
김밥

Flavor: Savory, slightly sweet, nutty

A convenient Korean rice roll wrapped in seaweed, perfect for lunch, snacks, or picnics. It’s a popular choice for school lunches and outdoor trips.

Bulgogi
불고기

Flavor: Sweet, savory, smoky

Thinly sliced beef marinated in soy sauce, garlic, sugar, and sesame oil, then grilled or stir-fried. Often served with rice and lettuce wraps.

Japchae
잡채

Flavor: Savory, slightly sweet, chewy

A stir-fried dish made with glass noodles, vegetables, and beef or mushrooms, seasoned with soy sauce and sesame oil.

Bungeoppang
붕어빵

Flavor: Crispy, warm, slightly sweet

A crispy, golden-brown pastry filled with sweet red bean paste, custard, or chocolate, shaped like a fish. It’s a popular winter street snack.

Yakgwa
약과

Flavor: Sticky, floral, lightly spiced

A traditional honey-glazed, deep-fried cookie made with wheat flour, sesame oil, and honey. It’s chewy, fragrant, and often served during special occasions like Korean holidays (Chuseok & Seollal).

Tteokbokki 떡볶이

Flavor: Spicy, sweet, chewy

A popular Korean street food made of chewy rice cakes simmered in a thick, spicy, and slightly sweet gochujang-based sauce. Often enjoyed as a snack or comfort food.

Naengmyeon カツカレー

Flavor: Refreshing, tangy, slightly sweet

A cold Korean noodle dish, served in icy broth or spicy sauce, perfect for summer or after BBQ. Originally from North Korea, it’s a must-try at Eulmildae or Hamheung-style spots!

Bokkeumbap볶음밥

Flavor: Savory, slightly spicy, umami-rich

Stir-fried rice with vegetables, egg, and protein, often flavored with soy sauce, gochujang, or kimchi. A classic Korean comfort dish.
